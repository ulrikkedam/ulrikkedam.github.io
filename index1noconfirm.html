Smart Home Assistant Study

Short interaction study on how a smart-home assistant moves from asking to suggesting to acting on its own.

Instructions

Imagine you have a smart-home assistant that controls your window, ceiling fan, curtains, and indoor light. The assistant learns from your repeated choices — over time it may stop asking every time and start acting more on its own.

You will go through two short scenario blocks:

🌡️ Block 1 — Temperature: The room feels warm. The assistant can open the window or turn on the fan.
💡 Block 2 — Lighting: The room feels dark. The assistant can open the curtains or turn on the light.

In each block you will also see a situation where the assistant acts on its own — without you saying anything first — because it has seen the same conditions before. You will be asked whether that felt appropriate.

The sensor bar at the top always shows what the assistant can see: room conditions, outdoor context, and the current state of each device.
